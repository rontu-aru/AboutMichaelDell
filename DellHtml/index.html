Michael Saul Dell has been, and is, one of the front runners in the evolution and over all development of the computer science field. However, he didn’t start out as a brilliant entrepreneur or business executive. He got his start in business by selling computer disk drives out of his college dorm room. After his small, almost miniscule business gained momentum, he moved out of the dorms and filled an apartment with a whole host of computer parts and building new computers to sell. But building and selling computers is not the only contribution that Dell provided, rather, Dell Inc. fundamentally pioneered Internet commerce. Which, essentially entails, “the use of the Internet for all phases of creating and completing business transactions.”
